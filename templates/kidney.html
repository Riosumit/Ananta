About kidneyTransplant
"The greatest gift you can give to someone is the gift of life. Be an organ donor"

Kidney transplant is a medical procedure that involves replacing a diseased or damaged kidney with a healthy one from a donor. In India, kidney transplant is a common procedure, with many hospitals offering world-class facilities and expertise.

Precautions to be taken before donation:
  • Complete medical examination
  • Blood test to determine blood type and compatibility
  • Psychological evaluation
Precautions to be taken at the time of donation:
  • Maintaining proper hydration
  • Monitoring vital signs
  • Following proper surgical protocols
Precautions to be taken after donation:
  • Strict adherence to medications
  • Regular monitoring of kidney function and blood pressure
  • Avoiding certain medications and foods
Maintaining a healthy lifestyle:
  • Adequate intake of protein
  • Avoiding foods high in potassium, phosphorus, and sodium
  • Drinking plenty of fluids
  • Eating a variety of fruits and vegetables

After a successful kidney transplant, the recipient's quality of life can improve significantly. They may experience an increase in energy, improved appetite, and a reduction in symptoms associated with kidney failure, such as fatigue and muscle cramps. However, there are also possible side effects that the recipient may experience after the transplant, such as

  • Infection: Due to the suppression of the immune system, the recipient is at a higher risk of infections

  • Rejection: Rejection occurs when the recipient's immune system identifies the new kidney as a foreign object and attacks it. Symptoms of rejection may include fever, tenderness over the transplanted kidney, and a decrease in urine output.

  • Medication side effects: The medications that the recipient must take to prevent rejection may cause side effects such as high blood pressure, high cholesterol, and diabete
